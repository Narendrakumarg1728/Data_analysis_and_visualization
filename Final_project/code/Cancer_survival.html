Is Cancer Deadly?

Estimated cancer deaths in USA alone is around 600,000 and 1.5 Million new cases were diagnosed in 2016. When a person is diagnosed with cancer the first question that occurs to them is “why me?” then “am I going to die?” and “how long do I have?”

So, does every cancer cause death? Does the mortality rate differ based on gender? Is there a difference in mortality between developed and other under developed countries?

Can we do something?

From the second graph "5 Year Survival Rate at different stages" we can clearly see that the survival rate drastically decreases if the cancer is detected in later stages for Colorectum cancer in men and Breast cancer in women.

So it is recommended for women to perform regular "self examination" of the breast and if there is any changes in the look and feel, it is recommended to perform screening tests like mammogram. Also "American cancer society" recommends the below periodicty of screening for women.
Women between 40 and 44 have the option to start screening with a mammogram every year. Women 45 to 54 should get mammograms every year. Women 55 and older can switch to a mammogram every other year, or they can choose to continue yearly mammograms.

Similarly "American Cancer Society" recommends to perform regular "Colorectal Cancer Screening Tests" which will help to find the cancer in early stages.
